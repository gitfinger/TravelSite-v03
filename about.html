About

It used to be hard for tourists to find out about much of the world; to have an idea what's there and whether it's worth the effort to see it. These days there are so many sites for tour operators, hotel and transport providers as well as amateur bloggers, trip advisers and so on that it is hard to know where to start and which to trust.

Traditional guide books like Lonely Planet, Rough Guide and Bradt can help of course, though they feel targeted at back-packers, carefully advising how to cut costs. Occasionally, newspaper travel sections cover somewhere more interesting, but they often seem like thinly-veiled PR that end up recommending you book package tours and all-inclusive hotels.

To cut through all this noise, Broader Horizons aims to offer an authoritative introduction to more adventurous destinations. We are not reckless - we would never suggest going anywhere you might get into danger - but we do want to encourage you to try something further afield and provide some signposts to help your research. We expect you want to travel in style, with the best facilities available. And while we know you are looking for advice, we respect that you also want to create your own trips independently. This website is a starting point.

Broader Horizons is edited by Simon Buckby and it depends on experienced travellers with no ties to commercial interests. We are intending to build our stock of destination reviews cautiously and carefully. We value reliable judgment, intelligent insight and high quality writing.

If you feel you have these qualities, have just come back from somewhere novel and want to tell like-minded people why they should go there - what they'll experience and what the barriers are to getting at it - then please contact us.

Or if you have been somewhere we've written about and want to add a few pointers, take issue with what we've said, or just upload your thoughts and photos under the relevant country section, then please feel free.
